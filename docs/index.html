Machine Learning for
Ancient Languages

ACL 2024 Workshop
Thailand, August 15-16 2024

The ML4AL Workshop aims to inspire and support research momentum in the emerging field of ML for the study of ancient texts.

Ancient languages preserve the cultures and histories of the past.
However, their study is fraught with difficulties, and experts must tackle a range of challenging text-based tasks, from deciphering lost languages to restoring damaged inscriptions, to determining the authorship of works of literature.

Technological aids have long supported the study of ancient texts, but in recent years advances in Artificial Intelligence (AI) and Machine Learning (ML) have enabled analyses on Ancient Languages on an unprecedented scale and in unparalleled detail. This shift is reminiscent of how scientific instruments such as microscopes and telescopes have contributed to the domain of Science.

  • When

    August 11th to 16th, 2024.

  • Where

    Bangkok, Thailand.

  • Registration

    Refer to the official ACL 2024 website.

The Opportunity

Our prior work (Sommerschield et al., 2023) has tracked an unmistakable research momentum across the ever-increasing number of published studies focused on ML for ancient languages. By surveying over 300 articles, we were able to identify a steady rise in interest in this topic over the last five years.

This is mainly due to the greater availability of data and fast-paced scientific progress. Competitions and workshops were important contributors in amplifying this trend. This is therefore a unique moment and a remarkable opportunity for interdisciplinary research, and the high quality of the ACL conference will allow ML4AL to further maximise its impact on the field.
